======================================================================
CALIBRATION CAVEATS
======================================================================

The structural-junction detector uses an absolute threshold of 20 high-
density elements within the probe radius to identify load-routing
junctions. This threshold was calibrated against the bumper case study
(mesh spacing ~3 mm). On parts with substantially coarser meshes (spacing
> 6 mm), this threshold may not be reached even at genuine junctions,
causing the detector to miss them.

If your part has a coarser mesh than ~3 mm spacing: the structural
filter's OTHER checks (FoS adjacency, critical-zone proximity) still fire
correctly, but specifically the junction detector may under-trigger.
Manually verify removal regions at visually identifiable rib/wall
intersections before trusting any 'not flagged' verdict in those areas.

Similarly: the tunnel detector uses an absolute aspect ratio of 2.5:1 and
asymmetry of 3.0x. These are calibrated to typical automotive-component
scales. Substantially different scales may need re-calibration.

Statistical-floor calibration (max of absolute_floor and per-part
statistic) was investigated but requires further validation; this is
articulated as concrete future work.
